What is CGST, SGST and IGST?

GST in India is divided into three components depending on the type of transaction:

  • CGST (Central GST): Collected by the Central Government on intra-state transactions. Equal to half the GST rate.
  • SGST (State GST): Collected by the State Government on intra-state transactions. Equal to half the GST rate.
  • IGST (Integrated GST): Collected by the Central Government on inter-state transactions. Equal to the full GST rate.

Example: For an 18% GST intra-state transaction — CGST = 9% and SGST = 9%. For inter-state — IGST = 18%.

How to calculate GST

Adding GST (exclusive price): GST Amount = Original Price × GST Rate ÷ 100. Final Price = Original Price + GST Amount.

Removing GST (inclusive price): Original Price = Inclusive Price ÷ (1 + GST Rate ÷ 100). GST Amount = Inclusive Price – Original Price.

Frequently Asked Questions

To add 18% GST: multiply the original price by 0.18 to get the GST amount, then add it to the original price. Example: ₹1,000 × 0.18 = ₹180 GST. Final price = ₹1,180. Use our calculator above — enter ₹1,000, select 18% and click Calculate.
To remove 18% GST from an inclusive price: divide the price by 1.18. Example: ₹1,180 ÷ 1.18 = ₹1,000 (original price). GST = ₹180. Use the "Remove GST" mode in our calculator above for instant results.
Restaurant GST in India: Non-AC restaurants charge 5% GST (no ITC). AC restaurants charge 5% GST. Hotels with room tariff above ₹7,500 charge 18% GST on food. Standalone outdoor catering charges 18% GST. Always check your bill — the GST rate should be mentioned clearly.
CGST goes to the Central Government and SGST goes to the State Government. Both are charged on intra-state (within the same state) transactions. The rate is split equally — for 18% GST, CGST = 9% and SGST = 9%. For transactions between different states, only IGST at the full rate applies.
Items exempt from GST (0% slab) include: fresh fruits and vegetables, milk, eggs, bread, salt, stamps, judicial documents, printed books, maps and newspapers. Essential food items are generally exempt or in the 5% slab to keep them affordable for all citizens.
Freelancers providing services in India pay 18% GST on their invoices. However, GST registration is mandatory only if annual turnover exceeds ₹20 lakh (₹10 lakh for North-Eastern states). If your turnover is below the threshold, you are exempt from GST registration. Always consult a CA for your specific situation.
